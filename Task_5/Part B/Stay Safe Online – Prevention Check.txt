				Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist (for College Students in India)



Staying safe online is essential in today’s digital world, especially for students who frequently use online platforms for learning, communication, and payments. The following practical steps can help reduce the risk of cybercrime:

1.Keep your confidential information private—never share OTPs, passwords, or UPI PINs with anyone.

2.Be cautious with payment requests on apps such as Google Pay and PhonePe; approving a request means you are sending money.

3.Avoid scanning unknown QR codes, particularly when asked to receive payments.

4.Verify the authenticity of websites and links before entering any personal or financial details.

5.Use strong and unique passwords, and enable two-factor authentication for added security.

6.Download applications only from official and trusted sources to prevent malware risks.

7.Refrain from using public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities such as banking or online transactions.

8.Regularly update your device and applications to stay protected against security vulnerabilities.

9.Be mindful of what you share online, especially on social media platforms.

10.Always log out from shared or public devices after use.

Reporting Channels:
If you encounter or suspect cybercrime, report it immediately through the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or call the helpline 1930.
